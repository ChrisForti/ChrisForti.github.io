After spending over 10 years, and counting, as a yacht carpenter honing my craft. I've embarked on an exciting career shift into the world of devops, and software devolopment. Though worlds apart, I've found that many of the same principles that lead to high-quality boatbuilding. Continuous improvement, automation, and modular architectures, also enable scaling and resilience in complex software systems. My journey from physical to digital construction has been fueled by a passion for optimization, and capability building.

|Yacht portfolio|

Just as I iterated, wooden hull designs, implementing old & new world craftmanship, and techniques to improve structural integrity. All while keeping the vessel light, fast, and maneuverable. Now get applied to infrastructures and deployment pipelines. I may have traded routers, tablesaws and hand-planers for Bash, shell-scripting, and version source control...... Still, that builder's spirit remains.

Understanding the full software development life cycle (SDLC) and Agile methodologies has been an eye-opening experience. While I was previously focused on the deployment and maintenance phases, I now have a deep appreciation for the intricate processes involved in requirements gathering, design, and coding. Collaborating with cross-functional teams and iterating through sprints has become second nature. Applying my problem-solving skills from naval architecture to software development challenges has been incredibly rewarding. Breaking down complex requirements into smaller, manageable components and developing algorithms to solve them feels like a natural extension of my previous work. The analytical mindset required in both domains has been a significant asset.
